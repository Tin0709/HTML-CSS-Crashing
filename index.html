Bananas are berries, but strawberries aren't! In botanical terms, a berry is a fruit produced from the ovary of a single flower with seeds embedded in the flesh. So, bananas qualify because they meet these criteria, while strawberries, despite their name, are considered accessory fruits because their seeds are on the outside.
